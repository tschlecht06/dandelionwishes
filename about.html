I believe every child deserves a space where they feel truly seen.

Cat Ianni-Schlecht is a Licensed Professional Counselor and Registered Play Therapist based in Thornton, Colorado. She is passionate about working with children, adolescents, and families using play therapy — a research-supported approach that meets children where they are, using play as their natural language.

Cat began her career working as a School Counselor before making the transition to full time therapist. Her background working in schools has given her experience working with youth who have experienced trauma, loss, family changes or who are working on building self-esteem, addressing anxiety and learning social skills.

Cat is very passionate about working with neurodivergent individuals with autism, ADHD, sensory disorders, developmental disabilities and learning disabilities.

Cat approaches every session with warmth, curiosity, and a deep respect for each child's unique experience. She works collaboratively with parents throughout the process, helping families build stronger connections and lasting skills.

When not working, Cat loves to spend time with her two dogs (Woody and Emmie) and travel. She is on a mission to visit every National Park and eat all of the best ice cream in the US.

My Approach

Why play therapy?

Play is a child's language

Children naturally express thoughts and feelings through play. It's how they make sense of the world — and how therapy can reach them most effectively.

Safety comes first

A child can only grow when they feel truly safe. Cat creates a consistent, accepting environment where kids can explore without fear of judgment.

Parents as partners

Healing happens beyond the playroom too. Cat works closely with parents, sharing insights and tools to extend growth into everyday family life.

Every child is different

No two children or families are the same. Cat tailors her approach to each child's needs, temperament, and goals — never one-size-fits-all.
